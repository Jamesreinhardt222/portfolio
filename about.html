About Me

Island lover, serial nerd, and fitness enthusiast

BIO

Born in New York, I moved to Vancouver Island when I was very little. My parents are bike mechanics, so I spent most of my childhood mountain biking. You can often find me hanging out by the river with my friends.

British Columbia Institute of Columbia

Full Stack Developer

I'm currently studying software development in BCIT's computer systems technology program. It is a very hands-on, practical program that focuses on real-world skills such as agile-development, professional documentation, and industry-standard programming practices.

University of Victoria

Combined major in Biology and psychology

Being well-rounded is important to me, so I took courses in philosophy, genetics, biochemistry, computer science, psychology, and Batman Studies (my favourite). I got my first taste of programming by using R to run statistical tests. I volunteered at hospitals and psych wards during my time there, and after graduating I went to work at a homeless shelter.

Blue Coast Research

Sample Prep Technician

I wanted research experience for medical school, so I worked for Blue Coast Research in Parksville. Their software development team creates computer-generated models of underground gold and silver mines. I would measure the particle size distribution of samples and prepare them for further analysis to find out their gold and silver content.
